what are nerve cells?

Nerve cells, or neurons, are the basic units of the nervous system responsible for the transmission of information throughout the body. Neurons have a unique structure consisting of a cell body, dendrites (short projections for receiving signals), and axons (long projections for sending signals). Neurons communicate through electrical impulses and chemical signals, enabling the coordination of various body functions, such as movement, sensation, and thinking. Neurons also form complex networks that allow the brain and nervous system to work efficiently.

what is the optic nerve

The optic nerve is a bundle of nerve fibers that connects the eye to the brain. Its function is to send visual information from the retina in the eye to the brain to be processed into images. The optic nerve passes through the optic chiasma, where some of the fibers from each eye cross, allowing integration of visual information from both eyes. Disorders of the optic nerve can affect vision or cause blindness.
